Nilambur Stories

Nilambur is not just a town; it is a story written in teak leaves and river songs. From its royal heritage and historic plantations to its modern eco-tourism initiatives, Nilambur stands as a perfect blend of history and nature.

Nilambur – Old Stories & New Stories

Nilambur, often called the “Land of Teak Plantations,” is a beautiful town in Malappuram district, Kerala. Surrounded by lush forests and flowing rivers, Nilambur is a place where history and nature live together in harmony.

Old Stories of Nilambur

1. The Royal Legacy – Nilambur Kovilakam

The history of Nilambur is closely connected with the royal family of Nilambur Kovilakam. During the British period, the royal family played a major role in protecting forests and promoting teak cultivation. Because of this effort, Nilambur became famous as a major center for teak plantations in India.

2. Conolly's Plot – The World’s First Teak Plantation

In the 1840s, British Collector H. V. Conolly started one of the world’s first planned teak plantations here. Even today, visitors can see teak trees that are over 150 years old. Conolly’s Plot remains one of Nilambur’s proud historical landmarks.

3. Chaliyar River – The Lifeline of Nilambur

The Chaliyar River is the heart of Nilambur. In earlier times, massive teak logs were transported through the river by floating them downstream. The river supported the livelihoods, culture, and daily life of the local people for generations.

4. Adyanpara Waterfalls – Nature’s Hidden Treasure

Adyanpara Waterfalls is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in Nilambur. Surrounded by rocky hills and forests, it becomes especially powerful and breathtaking during the monsoon season. It is not just a tourist spot, but also a symbol of Nilambur’s raw natural beauty.
